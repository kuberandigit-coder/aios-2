Data Sources & Calculation Rules
Product-level source: PostgreSQL staging_ai.cppc_workbook_product_performance_v1 — confirmed genuinely product-level (9,672 distinct products across 9,673 total rows in the full table). Filtered to campaign IDs 20763699505, 23684789991, 23053104908, 23431543574 (4 of Mahima's 5 ledsone.de campaigns with product-level rows; confirmed via staging_ai.cppc_campaign_truth_registry_v1, owner = "Mahi").
Product Title & Price: joined from public.google_merchant_products on google_item_id = product_id, restricted to ledsone.de's 4 merchant IDs AND country = 'DE' to guarantee exactly one matching row per product (see Known Limitations for why this restriction was necessary). Matched for 127 of 680 rows (19%) — the rest show "Data Missing", not a guess.
CTR = Clicks ÷ Impressions · Avg CPC = Cost ÷ Clicks · Conv. Rate = Conversions ÷ Clicks · ROAS = Conv. Value ÷ Cost. All computed directly from the source row's spend/clicks/impressions/conversions/revenue fields. Read-only SQL only — no data modified.
Known Limitations
1. Data is not live “last 30 days from today.” The freshest available data across every Google Ads/product table checked in PostgreSQL is dated 2026-06-11 (~4 weeks old). No Google Ads or Google Merchant Center API connector is available in this environment to pull live data; connecting one would require a Google-approved Developer Token and OAuth setup (see handover for details).
2. Product Price matched for only 127 of 680 rows (19%). The Merchant Center feed has up to 15 duplicate rows per product across different marketing "feed_label" segments (e.g. BLACKFRIDAY, TOPSALE, AOVU15), each carrying a different price for the same product (spot-checked example: one product had prices ranging £6.49–£13.37 across 15 feed-segment rows for the identical item). Restricting to the canonical country = 'DE' row avoids picking an arbitrary/wrong price, but most products don't have that specific row populated — shown honestly as Data Missing rather than guessed.
3. Product Cost, Gross Profit, and Profit After Ads are Data Missing for every row. No table anywhere in the database has real populated product-cost/COGS values (checked development.sku_cogs — empty; staging_ai.cppc_cogs_truth_model_v1 — all cost fields NULL for these products, and separately confirmed via staging_ai.google_feed_field_discovery_v1 that internal_sku coverage in the cogs model is only 11.99% site-wide). Gross Profit and Profit After Ads cannot be calculated without real cost data and are not invented.
4. Feed Status and Missing Attribute are Data Missing. The correct source table (raw_data.gmc_product_diagnostics_daily) is completely empty (0 rows) — no Feed Status/Missing Attribute data exists anywhere in the database.
5. Last 7 Days ROAS / Last 30 Days ROAS are not shown separately. The source table stores one aggregate window per row with no day-level history to compute a rolling comparison from.
6. Campaign coverage: only 4 of Mahima's 5 active ledsone.de campaigns have product-level rows in the source table; “Shopping DE | Mahi | klarna | TOP-MAHI | Verkaufsprodukt | tROAS | 11/06” (campaign 23926509987) has campaign-level totals but no product-level breakdown available.
7. Suggested Action rule not independently verified — see note above the table.
